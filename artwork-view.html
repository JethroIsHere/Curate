Liberty Leading the People

c. 1830

Artist: Eugène Delacroix

Medium: Oil on Canvas

Movement: Romanticism

Overview

Painted by Eugène Delacroix in 1830, this monumental work commemorates the July Revolution in Paris, which overthrew the oppressive regime of King Charles X. At the center of the chaotic barricades stands an allegorical personification of Liberty. Wearing the red Phrygian cap of freedom and raising the French Tricolor flag, she leads a diverse uprising of citizens—ranging from the wealthy bourgeoisie to factory workers and students—through the smoke-filled streets toward a new republic.

Sources: Encyclopædia Britannica, EBSCO Research
